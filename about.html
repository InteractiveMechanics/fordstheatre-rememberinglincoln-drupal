About the project

The assassination of President Lincoln on April 14, 1865 shocked the nation. Less than a week after the surrender of the Confederacy, the United States of America had only begun to consider what the end of the war might mean for the future of the reunited country. Public reaction varied widely from grief, to confusion, to celebration. 150 years later, what can we learn from the reactions and reflections of citizens from across the nation?
